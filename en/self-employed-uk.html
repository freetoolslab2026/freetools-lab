How much tax do I pay as self-employed in the UK?
You pay income tax on taxable profits after your personal allowance, plus Class 4 National Insurance on profits above the lower profits limit. If your adjusted net income is above £100,000, your personal allowance is tapered, creating a very high marginal tax zone.

Frequently asked questions

Class 4 NI is charged on self-employed profits above the lower profits limit. For 2026/27, the main rate is 6% between £12,570 and £50,270, then 2% above that.
