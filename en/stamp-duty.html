How much stamp duty will I pay?
The amount depends on where the property is located, the purchase price and your buyer type. England and Northern Ireland use SDLT, Scotland uses LBTT and Wales uses LTT. Each system has different bands, and additional homes can trigger significant surcharges.

FAQ

Stamp duty is the common name for the tax paid when buying property. In England and Northern Ireland it is SDLT, in Scotland it is LBTT, and in Wales it is LTT.
For SDLT, all buyers must be first-time buyers and the price must be within the relief rules. Scotland has a smaller first-time buyer relief. Wales does not currently have a separate first-time buyer relief for LTT.
It is an extra charge when buying an additional residential property, such as a second home or buy-to-let. The rules and rates differ across SDLT, LBTT and LTT.
LBTT is Scotland’s property transaction tax. It has different bands from SDLT and an Additional Dwelling Supplement that applies to many additional residential purchases.
